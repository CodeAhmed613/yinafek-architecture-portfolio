Project Overview

The Wellness Center is a graduation thesis project exploring architecture as a tool for physical and mental restoration. The project creates a healing environment through natural elements, human-centered spaces, and connection with nature.

Project Type

Wellness / Healthcare Architecture

Location

Megenagna, Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

Academic

Architecture and Urban Planning
Unity University

Design Approach

Biophilic design, natural lighting, green spaces, and healing environments.

Design Philosophy

The design focuses on creating spaces where architecture supports wellbeing. Instead of treating healthcare only as a functional requirement, the project explores how light, nature, water, and spatial experience can influence human emotions and recovery.

Designer Profile

Yinafek Tesfaye

Architecture and Urban Planning Student

Unity University

Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

I am honored to present my architectural thesis project, Wellness Center. This project represents more than a building to me; it is a response to something I have personally witnessed.

In today's fast-paced world, I have seen how stress, anxiety, physical exhaustion, and emotional pressure affect people of all ages, including members of my own family. These experiences made me realize that healing is not only about medicine. Sometimes people need a place where they can breathe, reconnect with nature, restore their minds, and regain balance in their lives.

That realization became the foundation of my project. I designed this Wellness Center to create an environment where architecture itself contributes to healing. Every space is carefully planned to promote calmness, movement, social connection, and reflection through natural light, water, greenery, and human-centered design.

Rather than designing a conventional healthcare facility, my goal was to create a destination that supports preventive wellness — a place where people come not because they are sick, but because they want to live healthier, happier, and more balanced lives.
